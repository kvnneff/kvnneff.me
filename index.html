About

I'm a front-end and node.js developer living on a small homestead in rural Williams, OR.

In 2008 I joined Brown Paper Tickets as a weekend call-center representative and over the years pushed myself to learn and grow, advancing into titles such as Client Services Representative, Client Tech Support Representative, Client Tech Support Manager, Office Tech Support Representative and eventually I became a self-taught Full-Stack Developer.

For my last 3 years at Brown Paper Tickets I participated within a small team of developers and designers that worked to improve and expand the front-end and back-end of the Brown Paper Tickets website. I worked with TCL and PostgreSQL on the server and HTML, CSS and Javascript on the client side of things.

In my down time I experimented with node.js and a more modular approach to coding and became hooked. I had an idea to create a graphical user interface that would allow Brown Paper Tickets employees to easily create new seating charts for venues, a process that had previously been done by entering hundreds of rows of data manually into a spreadsheet and could take a single employee multiple days to finish.

Given full-reign to pursue this side project on my own, I leveraged the power of node.js, npm, and front-end packagers such as Browserify and Duo to create an HTML5 Canvas point & click interface that enabled Brown Paper Tickets employees to lay out a seating chart, name the rows and seats and calculate seat desirability in under an hour, saving Brown Paper Tickets hundreds of man-hours.

In 2015 I left Brown Paper Tickets to seek out more opportunities to work with codebases and projects that utilize Javascript and modularity on both the back-end and front-end.

In my free time I enjoy lifting weights, hunting, gardening, raising chickens and rabbits, and dungeon mastering a Shadow of the Demon Lord campaign for friends.

Recent Projects

Blox Party

A multiplayer Tetris game built with Deku (a React-like framework), Browserify and Electron. I created this in my free time in order to experiment with Web Sockets. Players connect to a centralized server which receives data from each connected client and redistributes that data to keep the game in sync.

Township Media

I was contracted by Civic Maker Lab to create a plugin for their CMS Township. The plugin allows Township authors to upload media files to be embedded in posts and has the capability to store the files either to the server's file system or to an AWS S3 bucket.
