Brown Paper Tickets (2008 - 2015)

Technologies: TCL, PostgreSQL, Javascript, CSS, node.js

In 2008 I joined Brown Paper Tickets as a weekend call-center representative and over the years pushed myself to learn and grow, advancing into positions the positions of Client Services Representative, Client Tech Support Representative, Client Tech Support Manager, Office Tech Support Representative and eventually I became a self-taught front-end and TCL developer.

My last 3 years at Brown Paper Tickets were spent within a small team of developers and designers that worked to improve and expand the front-end and back-end of the Brown Paper Tickets website. I worked with TCL and PostgreSQL on the server and HTML, CSS and Javascript on the front-end.

In my down time I experimented with node.js and I proposed creating an app with it that would allow Brown Paper Tickets employees to easily create new seating charts for venues, a process that had previously been done by entering rows of data manually into a spreadsheet and could take a single employee multiple days to finish.

I was given full-reign to pursue this project on my own an created an HTML5 Canvas point & click interface that enabled Brown Paper Tickets employees to lay out a seating chart, quickly apply meta-data to individual rows and seats, and use a Euclidean algorythm to automatically generate seat desirability.

The app was powered by a node.js server that stored the finished seating charts in a CouchDB database.
