In 2008 I joined Brown Paper Tickets as a weekend call-center representative and over the years pushed myself to learn and grow. I advanced into the positions of Client Services Representative, Client Tech Support Representative, Client Tech Support Manager, and Office Tech Support Representative before I was asked to join the development team.

My last 3 years at Brown Paper Tickets were spent within a small team of developers and designers that worked to improve and expand the front-end and back-end of the Brown Paper Tickets website. I worked with TCL and PostgreSQL on the server and HTML, CSS and Javascript on the front-end.

In my down time I experimented with Node.js and created a proposal for an app that would allow Brown Paper Tickets employees to easily create new seating charts for venues, a process that had previously been done by entering rows of data manually into a spreadsheet and would take a single employee multiple days to finish.

I was given full-reign to pursue this project on my own and created an HTML5 Canvas point & click interface that enabled Brown Paper Tickets employees to quickly lay out a seating chart, apply meta-data to individual rows and seats, and use a Euclidean algorythm to automatically generate seat desirability.

The app was powered by a Node.js server that stored the finished seating charts in a CouchDB database. The app state was distributed to each connected client using PouchDB/CouchDB's replication features so updates to a chart's data could be viewed by others in real-time.
